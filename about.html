There is no reason that the form paradigm should still exist. 

The problem of forms first became apparent to us at the beginning of last year. We found ourselves in and out of hospitals and financial institutions constantly filling out the same information: personal details, health records, medical aid, credit scores... The list goes on. 

In those private moments of anxiety, we felt ourselves being reduced to ticket numbers, as though our data were more important than our wellbeings. And this was (and is) a problem that spanned industries. Opening a brokerage account, joining the loyalty programs of brands we respected: every new connection was blocked by those little white boxes, online and on paper. 

And it wasn't just us; globally, drop-off rates have never been so high. It should be clear that forms are an inadequate solution to vendor-relationship management and to customer acquisition. And autofill certainly isn't an option in the physical world. It's barely an adequate solution for web interfaces with even minor layers of complexity. (Try getting a password manager to differentiate between your details and those of your spouse.)  

Enter slide

So what is our solution? To begin with, it's novel. We intend for slide to become the definitive API for personal information. If Stripe is dev/payments, then slide is dev/information. And nothing else exists like it. 

Slide centralizes all of its data with its users, allows them to establish relationships without forms, and connects them with all of their vendors (new and old) through mobile apps and web interfaces so that their personal data profile is perfectly up-to-date. Imagine this: (i) You establish and begin to build a slide profile. (ii) In seconds, you are able to join every student-run-organization and local business in your area. (iii) These various organizations push information back to you such as membership numbers and usage data which is cryptographically linked to their vendor accounts. (iv) You can use this information to establish new relationships (with full proof that your bank account details really are your bank account details. (v) When your data changes, those changes are automatically pushed to all of the vendors with whom you have established relationships. And throughout this process, your data is secured using cutting-edge technology, and you don't have to waste any time (normally at least fifty hours a week) filling in those little white boxes. 

So slide isn't an autofill platform, and it isn't a password manager. It can't be. All of those platforms require that forms exist in the first place. We don't. And it's about time.
